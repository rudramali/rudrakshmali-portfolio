Engineering Philosophy
"The most interesting engineering problems sit where technical decisions, manufacturing realities, cost constraints, and human factors intersect."

Good engineering is not just about finding a solution — it is about finding the right solution, built with an understanding of how it will be made, who will use it, and what constraints it must navigate. I approach every project as a system: components do not exist in isolation, and decisions made at the design stage have consequences that ripple through manufacturing, assembly, maintenance, and end use. This perspective — shaped by competitive motorsport, professional R&D, and postgraduate management study — is what I bring to every engineering challenge.
