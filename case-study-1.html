Formula Bharat 2023–24 · Team RGIT Racing

01 · Problem

The Challenge

Formula Student requires student teams to design, build, and compete with a single-seat formula-style race car. Beyond the engineering challenge, it demands exceptional project management, cross-team coordination, and the ability to make sound engineering decisions under time and budget pressure.

For Team RGIT Racing, the primary technical challenge was the chassis: the existing design was overweight, structurally inefficient, and limiting the car's performance in dynamic events. The target was a significant reduction in unsprung and total chassis mass without compromising structural integrity or driver safety — both critical requirements under Formula Student rules.

Simultaneously, as Team Captain, the challenge was leading 25 engineers across mechanical, electrical, and aerodynamics sub-teams — ensuring all systems integrated correctly and that the full car was competition-ready on schedule.

02 · Constraints

Engineering Constraints

  • Regulatory compliance: All chassis dimensions, materials, and construction methods governed by Formula Student rules (SAE / FSAE standards)
  • Safety requirements: Driver roll hoop, impact attenuator, and harness mounting points must meet strict load case requirements
  • Budget: Limited team budget required careful material selection and supplier negotiation
  • Timeline: 8-month build cycle from design freeze to competition day
  • Manufacturing capability: Constrained to in-house tube bending, TIG welding, and university workshop facilities

03 · Design Process

From Concept to Competition

The design process began with a thorough review of the previous season's chassis — identifying high-mass, low-stress regions that were candidates for material removal or tube gauge reduction. Load path analysis using hand calculations established the primary structural requirements before moving into CAD.

The chassis was fully modelled in SolidWorks, with tube centrelines defined first, followed by joint design. Design iterations were driven by FEA results, with each design change evaluated against a combination of mass, stiffness, and factor of safety.

Close collaboration with the suspension, drivetrain, and aerodynamics sub-teams was essential — hard point locations, firewall packaging, and bodywork mounting all influenced chassis geometry. Weekly technical reviews kept all teams aligned.

04 · Engineering Analysis

FEA-Driven Optimisation

ANSYS Mechanical was used to run a series of structural analyses across the key load cases: torsional stiffness, bending stiffness, front impact, side impact, and roll-over. Each analysis identified regions of stress concentration and under-utilised material.

The optimisation process was iterative: FEA results informed design changes, which were re-modelled in SolidWorks, re-meshed, and re-analysed. Key outcomes included:

  • Reduction in tube wall thickness on secondary structural members where stress results demonstrated significant margin
  • Reorientation of key bracing tubes to improve load path efficiency
  • Localised reinforcement at high-stress nodes, replacing mass removed elsewhere
  • Final chassis mass: 31 kg (down from 40 kg) with equal or improved stiffness-to-weight ratio

05 · Key Decisions

Engineering Judgement

Material grade selection for main hoop

Chosen to remain with AISI 4130 chromoly steel rather than switching to aluminium for the main roll hoop — the weldability, toughness, and rules-compliance considerations outweighed the mass saving from aluminium at this structural node.

Tube gauge standardisation

Standardised to two tube gauges (rather than four in the previous design) to simplify manufacturing and reduce cutting/bending setup time — accepting a minor mass penalty in exchange for a more reliable build schedule.

Budget allocation prioritisation

As Team Captain, made the decision to allocate a larger portion of the budget to driver ergonomics and pedal box design — reasoning that improved driver comfort translates directly to lap time in endurance events.

06 · Results

What We Achieved

The car competed at Formula Bharat — India's largest and most competitive Formula Student event — and achieved All India Rank 9, a significant improvement on previous seasons and a strong result against teams from India's premier engineering institutions.

The chassis optimisation delivered a 22.5% weight reduction with maintained or improved structural performance, validated through both FEA and physical testing. The car passed all technical scrutineering requirements first time.

07 · Lessons Learned

What I Took Away

01

Leadership is a technical skill. Managing 25 engineers effectively required as much precision as the FEA work — clear communication, structured decision-making, and the ability to resolve conflicting requirements across sub-teams.

02

Simulation guides but does not replace judgement. FEA results are only as good as the boundary conditions and assumptions behind them. Developing engineering intuition to sanity-check simulation outputs was essential.

03

Manufacturing constraints shape design. The best CAD model is worthless if it cannot be built reliably. Designing with the welding team's capabilities in mind led to a more consistent, higher-quality chassis.
