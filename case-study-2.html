Go Float Tech · Underwater Drone R&D · 2023

01 · Problem

The Challenge

Go Float Tech develops underwater drones for inspection and monitoring applications. The R&D challenge was to design components that could perform reliably in demanding aquatic environments — balancing hydrodynamic performance, structural integrity, watertight sealing, and manufacturability.

As an intern, I was assigned to the design and development of specific sub-components, working within a professional R&D environment with real production targets, real customers, and real engineering standards.

02 · Constraints

Engineering Constraints

  • Waterproofing: Components required sealing to defined ingress protection ratings
  • Weight and buoyancy: Material density a critical design parameter for neutral buoyancy targets
  • Manufacturability: Designs needed to be producible within the company's existing manufacturing processes
  • Integration: New components had to interface correctly with existing drone architecture and electronics
  • Cost: Unit cost constraints required design-for-manufacture thinking from the outset

03 · Design Process

From Brief to Production

01

Requirements capture

Worked with senior engineers to define functional requirements, interface constraints, and performance targets for each component before beginning any CAD work.

02

Concept generation and selection

Generated multiple concept solutions for each design challenge, evaluated against a weighted criteria matrix covering performance, cost, and manufacturability. Selected approaches were developed further in SolidWorks.

03

CAD development in SolidWorks

Developed detailed 3D models with full assembly context — checking fit, clearances, and interface geometry against adjacent components and the full drone assembly.

04

Prototype and test

Prototypes were produced and subjected to functional and environmental testing. Results were fed back into design iteration — adjusting geometry, tolerances, and material choices.

05

Production sign-off

Final designs reviewed and approved by the engineering team, then handed over to the manufacturing team for production integration. Multiple components passed this review and entered the production drone.

04 · Key Decisions

Engineering Judgement

Material selection for structural housings

Evaluated aluminium alloys vs engineering polymers for structural housings, considering corrosion resistance in salt water, machinability, and weight. Final selections balanced long-term durability with production cost targets.

Sealing strategy

Chose an O-ring face seal arrangement over alternative sealing methods for key access panels, prioritising field-serviceable reliability over a marginal weight saving from alternative approaches.

05 · Results

Commercial Impact

The most meaningful outcome of this internship was that multiple component designs were adopted directly into the production drone — not held for future consideration, but integrated into the product sold to customers. This represented a direct commercial contribution as an intern, validating both the technical quality of the designs and their manufacturing readiness.

The experience gave me first-hand understanding of how professional R&D teams operate: the rigour required at each stage of the design process, how engineering decisions are reviewed and challenged, and what it means to design something that actually gets built.

06 · Lessons Learned

What I Took Away

01

Professional R&D is iterative by nature. First concepts rarely make it to production unchanged. The discipline of systematic iteration — test, analyse, revise — is what separates professional engineering from student project work.

02

DFM must be considered from the first sketch. A design that cannot be manufactured reliably at cost is not a finished design. Manufacturability is a design requirement, not an afterthought.

03

Working in a professional team accelerates learning. Daily technical conversations with experienced engineers compressed years of learning into a single summer — the most valuable aspect of any internship.
